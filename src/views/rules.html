1. Checkers is played by two players. Each player begins with twelve pieces colored either black or red.
  2. The board that is made of 64 squares arranged in an 8 x 8 grid such that the color of the squares alternate between red and black.
  3. Both players twelve pieces are placed on the black squares such that the middle two rows contain no pieces.
  4. Players alternate turns.
  5. Pieces can only be moved to black squares in the direction opposite of the player.
  6. Pieces may only be moved one square away unless a valid jump is made.
  7. Pieces may be moved two squares away if the intermediate square is filled by an opponent's piece and the second square is vacant of pieces.
  8. When a piece is moved two squares, the opponent's piece in the intermediate square is removed from the board.
  9. A player must always choose moving two squares in favor of moving one.
  10. When a piece reaches the row furthest from its starting point it becomes a king.
  11. Kings are pieces that can move in any diagonal direction.
  12. The player wins when the opponent can no longer make a valid move.
